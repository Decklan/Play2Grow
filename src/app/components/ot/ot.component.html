Treatment Services

Sensory Integration

Sensory Integration treatment is designed to support an individual's ability to process and integrate sensory information so he or she can interact more comfortably with others; engage more successfully in movement, play, self-care, work, and learning activities; and better regulate social and emotional responses. Treatment is based on a thorough assessment of each person’s sensory-motor skills, and includes activities rich in movement, touch, deep pressure, auditory, and visual information; environmental modifications that support organized integration of sensory information; and a "sensory diet" of daily activities and routines designed to support organization of the nervous system.

Interactive Metronome

Interactive Metronome is a well-researched, computer-based intervention that focuses on improving processing abilities that affect attention, motor planning and sequencing. Research suggests that individuals who receive interactive metronome treatment show statistically significant changes in attention, organization, motor control, language processing, reading fluency and behavioral control – areas of developpment which are typically challenged in individuals with developmental, learning and attention problems such as ADHD, sensory processing disorders, nonverbal learning disorders, autism, learning disabilities and other related concerns. Therapeutic Listening

Therapeutic Listening Programs use sound in combination with sensory, movement, postural and breath work, and visual activities to support a variety of functional outcomes. All of our senses (touch, movement, smell, taste, hearing and vision) work together to help us understand and interact effectively with our world. In particular, sound combined with movement provides vital information about the orientation of the body in space and time that forms the basis of sensory motor function and perceptual organization. For this reason, listening programs incorporated into a sensory- motor treatment can result in positive changes in attention, organization of behavior, self-regulation, postural control, coordination of body sides, motor planning, fine and visual-motor skills, social engagement and communication. Autism Services

Autism services at Play to Grow Developmental Therapy integrate behavioral, relationship-based, social thinking, developmental approaches and communication supports into a comprehensive, individualized, family-friendly intervention program. With specialized training in the DIR/Floortime, the Early Start Denver Model (ESDM), Sensory Integration, Qiqong Sensory Massage, Social Thinking, The Play Project, the TEAACH Method, and visual instruction strategies and supports we have years of experience the resources to support your child and family.

Qiqong Sensory Massage

Qiqong has been practiced in China for thousands of years to promote general health and well-being. Qiqong sensory massage is a form of Qiqong practice that has been developed specifically for the needs of children with autism and sensory regulatory disorders that can be simply and effectively provided by parents in the home in approximately 15 to 20 minutes daily with training and support from a qiqong certified therapist. In addition to being a wonderful bonding experience between parent and child, research suggests that daily qiqong massage results in improvements in sensory processing; regulation of sleep, appetite and digestion; emotional regulation, social engagement, language skills, and motor development.
